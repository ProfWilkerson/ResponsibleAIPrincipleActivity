Principle or Violation? Short Case Gallery

Read each scenario, select the responsible AI principle most at issue, and decide whether the scenario upholds or violates that principle.

How to Use This Activity: For each of the 10 scenarios below, use the two dropdowns to (1) identify the responsible AI principle most at issue and (2) indicate whether the scenario upholds or violates that principle. When finished, click Check My Answers to get instant feedback. A full answer key is available at the bottom for reference.
Fairness Transparency Accountability Privacy Inclusiveness
1
Scenario

A tech company uses a hiring algorithm trained on its own historical resume data. The algorithm consistently ranks female applicants lower for software engineering roles, replicating patterns from decades of male-dominated hiring decisions.

2
Scenario

A hospital deploys an AI diagnostic assistant. After every recommendation, the system generates a plain-language summary explaining which symptoms and patient history factors most influenced its suggestion, which the care team shares with patients before any decision is made.

3
Scenario

A social media platform uses an AI content moderation system that removes posts and suspends accounts. Users receive no explanation for why their content was flagged and have no mechanism to appeal the AI's decision to a human reviewer.

4
Scenario

A popular smart home speaker continuously records ambient audio even when its wake word has not been spoken and shares these recordings with third-party advertisers. Users are not informed of this practice in the device's setup process.

5
Scenario

A city deploys an AI-powered public safety system to optimize emergency response times. The model was trained exclusively on incident data from high-income neighborhoods, resulting in significantly slower predicted response times in lower-income areas.

6
Scenario

A regional bank commissions an independent third-party audit of its AI loan approval model. The auditors' full report — including identified errors and corrective actions taken — is published on the bank's website and shared with regulators.

7
Scenario

A retail company uses a facial recognition system in its stores to identify suspected shoplifters. Customers are never informed through signage or any other means that their faces are being scanned and compared against a database when they enter the store.

8
Scenario

An insurance company's AI risk-scoring model is tested quarterly against outcomes data broken down by race, gender, and age. When disparate impact is detected in any group, the model is taken offline for retraining before it is used again in pricing decisions.

9
Scenario

A state government redesigns its benefits enrollment portal with an AI assistant that supports 14 languages, includes full screen-reader compatibility, and was co-designed with focus groups representing elderly, rural, and non-English-speaking residents.

10
Scenario

A mental health app uses AI to analyze users' therapy journal entries and session notes. Without users' knowledge or consent, this data — including diagnoses, medications, and personal disclosures — is sold to pharmaceutical research firms.
